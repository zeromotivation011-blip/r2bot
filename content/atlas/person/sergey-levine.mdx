Sergey Levine is a professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at UC Berkeley, and the co-founder of [robotics](/atlas/concept/robotics) startup Physical Intelligence. His research more or less defined how modern robots learn from data. If a humanoid in 2026 walks via [reinforcement learning](/atlas/concept/reinforcement-learning), or grasps an object via [imitation learning](/atlas/concept/imitation-learning), it's standing on Levine's papers.

## Why his work matters

Before about 2015, two big approaches dominated robotic learning:

- **Classical control** — write the math by hand for every [robot](/atlas/concept/robot) behaviour. Reliable but inflexible. A robot only does what's been explicitly programmed.
- **Reinforcement learning** — let the robot try and learn. Theoretically elegant but, in practice, took millions of attempts and barely worked on real robots.

Levine and his Berkeley group (BAIR — Berkeley AI Research) pushed a third approach: **deep reinforcement learning combined with imitation learning**. Show the robot demonstrations from humans (or simulated experts). Let it improve from there with RL. The combination dramatically reduced the amount of trial-and-error needed.

His 2015 paper "End-to-end training of deep visuomotor policies" was one of the first showing a robot could learn manipulation tasks (stacking blocks, fitting plugs) by [mapping](/atlas/concept/mapping) [camera](/atlas/concept/camera-vision) pixels directly to motor commands — no hand-engineered features in between. That paper is the foundation of what's now called **end-to-end robot learning**.

## What he's working on now (2026)

Levine co-founded **Physical Intelligence** (sometimes branded as π) in 2024, alongside Karol Hausman and others. The startup's mission: build a general-purpose AI brain that can transfer between any robot body. Backed by Jeff Bezos and others; the company has demonstrated VLA models running on a dozen different robotic platforms.

If you think of the humanoid race as a contest between "build the body" companies ([Tesla](/atlas/company/tesla), Figure, [Boston Dynamics](/atlas/company/boston-dynamics)) and "build the brain" companies, Physical Intelligence is the leading "build the brain" play.

## What to read

- *End-to-end training of deep visuomotor policies* (2015) — the foundational paper
- *RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models* (2023, Google DeepMind with Levine as advisor) — the VLA paradigm
- *Physical Intelligence π0 model* (2024) — the first cross-embodiment robot foundation model

His students and collaborators include Chelsea Finn (Stanford), Pulkit Agrawal (MIT), Karol Hausman (Physical Intelligence) — together this generation of researchers shapes most of the academic work robotics startups are now commercialising.

## Why this matters for the field

For the past decade, the bottleneck in robotics has shifted from hardware to software. The robots can move; the question is whether they can decide *what to do*. Levine's research arc — and his current commercial bet — is essentially the answer to that question. If general-purpose robot brains are possible, his pipeline of work is the most direct path there.
